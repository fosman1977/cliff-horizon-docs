The rain/work-stop peril is CH's primary launch peril. It covers construction operators, infrastructure developers, and outdoor contractors against days lost to excessive rainfall that halts work.

---

## Index definition

The index is a **count of rain days** in the contract period:

```
RainDays = count(days where precipitation ≥ threshold mm)
```

The default threshold is **10mm/day** (a standard construction work-stop threshold), but it is a dial — buyers can negotiate the threshold as part of the contract structure. The quote engine recomputes the full distribution for any threshold value.

Payout is triggered when `RainDays` exceeds the strike level in the contract period.

---

## Forward-climate calibration (QDM)

Historical raw ERA5 data is biased. CH applies **Quantile Delta Mapping (QDM)**, a trend-preserving bias-correction method:

1. Compute the monthly quantile map from ERA5 (1991–2014 calibration period) to the reference truth layer (IMD gauge for India, ERA5+NASA cross-check for Turkey).
2. Apply the map to the full ERA5 record including the validation period (2015–2024).
3. Extend to the contract horizon using CMIP6 ensemble projections.

QDM preserves the trend signal — if extreme precipitation is increasing, the forward distribution reflects that. History-based pricing does not.

**Calibration governance:** A change to the calibration method ships only if it lowers out-of-sample CRPS without degrading ECE or PIT. See [Calibration Workstream](/calibration).

## Settlement sources

| Geography | Primary | Cross-check | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| India | GPM IMERG Late V07B | ERA5 | Disclosed per site |
| Turkey | ERA5 | NASA POWER | Disclosed per site |
| Gulf | NASA POWER | ERA5/Open-Meteo | Disclosed per site |

The basis (gap between sources) is expressed in money — days × burnPerDay — and disclosed in every quote. Wide-basis sites are flagged with a QC warning.

## Product forms supported

Both **derivative** and **warranty** forms are available for rain/work-stop.

**Derivative:** Pays when the certified RainDays index exceeds the strike level. Ensuro is the capital wrapper.

**Warranty:** CH warrants the accuracy of its Forecasted RainDays against the certified Actual. Service credit formula:

```
SC = (|FA − AV| − VT) × WA,  capped at SC_max
```

The warranty form is what makes India transactable — it is a service-level agreement, not an insurance contract.

## Representative site: Rize, Turkey

Rize is on the Eastern Black Sea coast — one of the wettest locations in Turkey (~2,200mm/year). It is the most extreme example in the Turkey prospect geography.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Expected work-stop days/year at 10mm threshold | ~67 |
| Settlement source | ERA5 + NASA POWER |
| Basis (ERA5 vs NASA) | Disclosed per quote |
| Forms available | Derivative + warranty |

With ~67 work-stop days per year, Rize is near the upper bound of what is insurable — very few construction projects can absorb that loss without coverage. It is also the strongest demonstration of the pricing engine: the tail at Rize is materially different from the tail at Istanbul or Antalya.

## ERA5↔IMD basis (India)

For the Ashoka/Bankot site, ERA5 shows systematically higher wet-season accumulation than IMD gauge. This is the M1 calibration challenge. Until the ERA5→IMD re-targeting is resolved, the settlement is IMD gauge and the ERA5 is used only for the forward distribution. The basis is disclosed to the buyer.

<Note>
The ERA5↔IMD basis at Bankot is a known open item in M1 of the calibration workstream. It does not affect settlement (which is IMD / IMERG), but it affects the accuracy of the forward-climate premium estimate.
</Note>
