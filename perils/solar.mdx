Solar irradiance is available as a **warranty menu-line** on client request. It is not a launch wedge. This page explains why, and under what conditions it makes sense to serve a solar client.

---

## Why solar is not a wedge

The thesis requires a peril that creates meaningful economic risk for the "missing middle" buyer — an operator with concentrated exposure to a tail that capital can diversify. Solar fails this test at every timescale:

**Interannual stability:**
- Interannual coefficient of variation (CV) for GHI at Turkey and India sites: **2–6%**
- A solar farm with 5% revenue variance year-on-year does not have a tail problem. It has a budgeting problem.

**Monthly resolution is also thin:**
- At the 80%-trigger level used by InRisk Labs (the incumbent in this lane), the trigger fires in approximately **1/480 months** at the contract gridpoint
- This is not a product for the "missing middle" — it is a niche guarantee for project finance lenders

**The buyer's actual problem is financeability, not variance:**
- The buyers who purchase solar irradiance cover are doing so to satisfy lenders, not to protect against operational risk they feel
- The product being sold in the market (Marsh, Swiss Re, Arbol, InRisk, Tongwei) is a financeability/output-guarantee, not a variance hedge
- InRisk Labs already occupies this lane with a product tuned for Indian solar project finance

**Solar cannibalisation is structural:**
- High-irradiance days are also high-generation days for every solar farm simultaneously
- This creates a systematic power price depression on the days that should produce the most revenue
- This is not basis risk CH can solve — it is a structural feature of the market

---

## InRisk overlap

InRisk Labs offers a solar warranty product in India targeted at the same gridpoints and buyer profile. Competing directly on this lane means:

- Matching InRisk's existing product without differentiation
- Serving a buyer whose actual problem (lender covenants) is not well-served by a parametric index

This is a settled decision. Solar is killed as a CH wedge.

---

## When solar makes sense (menu-line)

Despite the above, CH will serve a solar client if:

1. The client specifically requests irradiance cover as part of a broader CH relationship
2. The exposure is bundled with rain, wind, or flood cover on the same site
3. The buyer is a CH warranty client already (India) and adds solar as a secondary line

In this case, CH uses ERA5 SSRD (surface solar radiation downwards) + NASA POWER GHI as the two-source index, with the same QDM forward calibration applied to other perils.
