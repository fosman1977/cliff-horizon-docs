Cliff Horizon is a **certified-index + forward-climate calibrated-pricing + structuring engine** for OTC parametric weather derivatives. It is not an insurer and not a data vendor. It is the pricing and structuring brain that sits between commercial buyers with concentrated weather risk and capital that needs diversified, uncorrelated lines.

**Ensuro** provides the capital and regulatory wrapper. CH is the non-principal index and calculation agent — market enabler, not market maker. Neutrality is the moat.

<Note>
**Assumption A — the current gate:** Mid-market commercial buyers will pay for OTC parametric cover and accept basis risk. Everything else blocks on this. The cheapest test is 3–5 buyer conversations at Turkey prospect locations using the demo funnel.
</Note>

## The moat

The moat is **forward-climate pricing × breadth × network** — not the index, not historical calibration, not forecasting.

| Moat layer | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Forward-climate pricing** | History-based pricing is insolvent on trending perils. CH prices on forward distributions (QDM + CMIP6), which is the only defensible method for a 5–10 year contract horizon. |
| **Breadth** | Each new uncorrelated peril/geography line makes capital's book more efficient. Capital follows breadth. Breadth creates a network of capital that can take each new line. |
| **Network** | Buyer relationships + capital relationships compound. Neither side talks to each other without a trusted intermediary. |

**What is not the moat:** data access (ERA5/NASA/IMERG are free), the index formula (simple and auditable by design), forecasting skill (no edge on public data — settled).

</Tip>
