Turkey is CH's **first prospect geography** — the location where Assumption A will be tested with real buyer conversations. 13 sites are live across rain, flood, and solar perils. There is no IMD-equivalent national gauge in Turkey, so all sites settle on ERA5 + NASA POWER cross-check with the basis disclosed in money.

---

## Why Turkey first

- **No single build season:** Construction work continues year-round, especially on the Eastern Black Sea coast. Rain risk is not seasonal — it is a year-round operational constraint.
- **Real events:** The August 2021 İstanbul flash flood (~97 deaths, ~$300M economic loss) and repeated Eastern Black Sea flooding (Artvin, Rize, Giresun) demonstrate that the tail events are real and recent.
- **Active construction pipeline:** Large infrastructure projects (roads, bridges, YEKA solar farms) create buyer candidates with concentrated weather exposure.
- **ERA5 settlement is credible:** In the absence of a nationally-trusted gauge (like India's IMD), ERA5 + NASA POWER cross-check is a reasonable settlement standard that buyers can verify independently.

## Eastern Black Sea rain sites

The five Eastern Black Sea sites (Rize, Trabzon, Artvin, Ordu, Giresun) are the strongest demonstration of the rain/work-stop product:

**Rize** is the most extreme example:
- Annual rainfall ~2,200mm
- Expected work-stop days at 10mm threshold: **~67 per year**
- A construction project with a 100-day window at Rize faces an expected 67 days of rain interruption — an existential schedule risk without cover

At these sites, the premium for a typical construction window is material and the buyer's alternative (bare contingency reserve) is expensive and illiquid.

---

## İstanbul August 2021 flood event

The August 2021 İstanbul flood is the anchor event for the flood peril in Turkey:

- >300mm of rainfall in 24 hours in parts of İstanbul
- ~97 deaths, approximately $300M in reported economic damage
- Affected the D-100 highway, metro tunnels, and multiple construction sites simultaneously

CH GEV fit for İstanbul places this event in the rare-but-plausible tail — not a 1-in-1000-year event, but a 1-in-30 to 1-in-100 depending on the specific gridpoint and source. This is within the insurable range.

## ERA5 ↔ NASA basis disclosure policy

Every Turkey quote discloses:

1. The two settlement sources (ERA5 and NASA POWER)
2. The basis between them expressed in equivalent downtime-days × burnPerDay
3. A QC flag if the basis exceeds a site-specific threshold

Wide-basis sites are flagged in the QC sweep (`/api/qc`). Buyers are briefed that the settled value will be from the designated primary source, and the cross-check source is disclosed as an independent reference.
