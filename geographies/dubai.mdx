Dubai and the broader Gulf is the primary target geography for the **flood derivative** product. The April 2024 extreme rainfall event crystallised the case: a disruptive flood event that affected hundreds of commercial operators, with no parametric product available to any of them.

---

## The April 2024 event

On 16 April 2024, an extreme rainfall event hit Dubai and the UAE:

- NASA POWER recorded approximately **53mm** at the Dubai gridpoint
- ERA5 / Open-Meteo recorded approximately **100mm** at the same point
- Station observations reported even higher values in some areas
- The event caused widespread operational shutdown across Dubai Airport (capacity reduced), free zones, construction sites, logistics operations, and retail

For context, Dubai's historical 1-in-100-year return level (ERA5 fit, pre-CC loading) is in the 100–150mm range. The April 2024 event was either a 1-in-45 or 1-in-130 event depending on which data source you use. Both estimates are disclosed to buyers as part of the basis discussion.

| Metric | Value |

Key return period estimates (pre-CC loading, ERA5):

| Event level | Return period |
|---|---|
| >25mm/24h (disruptive) | ~1-in-2 to 1-in-9 years |
| >50mm/24h (severe) | ~1-in-13 to 1-in-34 years |

**Independent validation:** Guy Carpenter / JBA EVA places Dubai City 1-in-125 at the 7-day 168mm level. CH's 1-in-132 for single-day severe events is directionally consistent with this benchmark.

## Two-source basis (disclosed)

| Source | Apr-2024 reading | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NASA POWER | ~53mm | GPM-family satellite+reanalysis |
| ERA5 / Open-Meteo | ~100mm | ECMWF reanalysis |
| Gap | 47mm | Buyer's acknowledged basis risk |

The 47mm gap is material. CH discloses this in every Dubai quote. The settlement source is specified in the contract — typically ERA5 as the primary source with NASA as the disclosed cross-check. A buyer who wants to use NASA POWER as settlement gets a different premium because the trigger frequency changes.

<Note>
Reanalysis is a floor. Station observations and radar consistently show higher intensities in extreme events. CH treats the reanalysis gap as a conservative bias — actual events are likely worse than the index records.
</Note>

## Indicative deal structure (free-zone operator)

The reference case for Dubai is a free-zone commercial operator (logistics, data centre, or light industrial) with measurable downtime costs per day of flood disruption.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Burn per day | AED 3,000,000 |
| Cap | 30 days |
| Maximum payout | AED 90,000,000 |
| Sweet spot flood-line | 50mm (1-in-13 to 1-in-34) |
| Indicative premium | AED 5.9–7.6m |
| Rate on line (RoL) | 7–8% |

The 50–70mm flood-line range is the sweet spot: frequent enough to be credible to the buyer, rare enough for capital to price below the buyer's pain threshold.

At a 25mm flood-line, the product is closer to business interruption insurance (too frequent, too cheap for capital). At 100mm, it is a catastrophe layer with very low trigger probability.
